# 🔗 Quantum Entanglement

"Spooky action at a distance" — Albert Einstein

## Understanding Quantum Entanglement

When two particles become one quantum system

### 🧪 The EPR Paradox

In 1935, Einstein, Podolsky, and Rosen published a famous paper arguing that quantum mechanics must be incomplete. They described entangled particles — pairs that share a quantum state such that measuring one **instantly** determines the state of the other, regardless of distance. Einstein called this **"spooky action at a distance"** and believed it proved quantum mechanics was missing some "hidden variables."

### 🔔 Bell's Theorem & Bell Tests

In 1964, physicist **John Bell** devised a mathematical inequality that could distinguish between Einstein's "hidden variables" and true quantum entanglement. Decades of experiments — culminating in the 2022 Nobel Prize-winning work — have consistently violated Bell's inequality, proving that quantum entanglement is *real* and that Einstein's local hidden variables cannot explain nature.

### ⚡ What You're Seeing

The moment you measure one particle, **both** wavefunctions collapse — particle A's measurement instantly determines B's result, even if they were on opposite sides of the universe!

### 🚀 Real-World Applications

- **🔐 Quantum Cryptography**: Entanglement enables theoretically unbreakable encryption through quantum key distribution (QKD).
- **💻 Quantum Computing**: Entangled qubits perform calculations impossible for classical computers, enabling exponential speedups.
- **📡 Quantum Teleportation**: Entanglement allows the quantum state of a particle to be "teleported" to a distant location.

### ❌ No Faster-Than-Light Communication

Despite the "instant" correlation, entanglement **cannot** be used to send messages faster than light. Measuring particle A gives you a random result — you can't control what you get. Only when you compare notes with the person who measured B (which requires classical communication) do you discover the correlation. Einstein's special relativity remains safe!
