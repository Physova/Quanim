# ⚛️ Quantum Superposition

Schrödinger's cat and wavefunction collapse

## Understanding Quantum Superposition

Where particles exist in all possible states at once

### 🐱 Schrödinger's Cat

In 1935, physicist Erwin Schrödinger proposed a famous thought experiment: imagine a cat in a sealed box with a radioactive atom that has a 50% chance of decaying. If the atom decays, a mechanism releases poison, killing the cat. According to quantum mechanics, until someone opens the box and observes, the atom exists in a **superposition** of both decayed and not-decayed states — and therefore the cat is both **alive and dead simultaneously**!

### 🎲 The Measurement Problem

Quantum particles genuinely exist in multiple states until **measurement** forces them to "choose." This isn't just uncertainty about which state they're in — they're mathematically in *all states at once*, with each state having a certain probability amplitude. Measurement causes what physicists call **wavefunction collapse**.

### 🔮 Quantum Computing

Superposition is the foundation of quantum computing. While classical bits are either 0 or 1, quantum bits (**qubits**) can be in superposition of both states simultaneously. This allows quantum computers to explore many solutions in parallel, enabling exponential speedups for certain problems like factoring large numbers or simulating molecules.
